Privacy Policy

Kip

Effective date: [EFFECTIVE DATE]

Kip is a chat app for talking to your own personal AI agent. The AI itself doesn't run on our servers — it runs either on a computer you own (through a companion app called Noggin that you download separately) or fully on your iPhone using Apple's on-device Apple Intelligence. Kip is just the window into that agent.

We don't collect your data

We — the developer — never receive your messages, photos, or anything else you send to Kip. When you chat, your messages go straight to your own Noggin server, or stay entirely on your device if you're using on-device mode. There is no middle server of ours in between.

Kip has no analytics, no tracking, no ad SDKs, and no account system that reports back to us. That's why the App Store privacy label for Kip says "Data Not Collected."

What happens fully on your device

Some things Kip does never leave your iPhone, regardless of whether you're connected to a Noggin server:

  • Image redaction — blurring faces or text in photos, using Apple's on-device Vision framework.
  • OCR (text recognition in photos) — also done on-device with Vision.
  • Voice dictation — turning your speech into text using on-device speech recognition.
  • On-device AI mode — when you choose "Continue without a server," your conversation runs entirely through Apple Intelligence on your iPhone.

Permissions Kip asks for, and why

iOS asks you to approve each of these individually, and you can change your mind anytime in Settings → Kip. Here's exactly why Kip requests each one:

  • Camera

    To scan the pairing QR code when connecting to your Noggin server, and to take photos to attach to messages.

  • Local Network

    To connect to your Noggin server when it's running on the same Wi-Fi network as your iPhone.

  • Microphone & Speech Recognition

    For voice dictation in chat.

  • Photo Library

    To attach photos to messages, and to save images back to your library when you ask Kip to.

  • Calendars, Reminders & Contacts

    Only used when you explicitly ask your agent to find, create, update, or delete a calendar event, reminder, or contact. Kip doesn't read these in the background.

  • Notifications

    To let you know when a long-running task finishes, or when a new message arrives.

Data your own Noggin server may store

If you connect Kip to a Noggin server, that server (running on a computer you control) may keep a local history of your conversations so your agent has memory and context. That storage lives on your computer, under your control — we don't have access to it, and it's outside the scope of this policy. Check the Noggin project's own documentation if you want details on how it stores data locally.

Children

Kip is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect information from anyone — children included, since we don't collect data at all.
