# United Kingdom and Ireland Activation Checklist

Status: **INACTIVE / BLOCKED**

Activation owner: Brandon

Last source check: 2026-07-15

The United Kingdom and Ireland are separate legal regimes. Ireland is also an EEA
member, so the [EEA checklist](EEA-ACTIVATION.md) applies in full to Ireland.
Completing one country does not activate the other.

## Shared readiness

- [ ] Freeze exact countries, products, providers, purposes, data locations, and
      cross-border transfers.
- [ ] Confirm territorial scope for both UK GDPR and EU GDPR.
- [ ] Map lawful basis per processing purpose; do not treat Terms acceptance as
      consent for all processing.
- [ ] Complete controller/processor roles, records of processing, retention,
      security, deletion, rights, complaints, and breach procedures.
- [ ] Assess whether a data protection officer or data protection impact
      assessment is required; document the result and mitigations.
- [ ] Verify special-category data handling for free-form prompts, voice, files,
      repositories, diagnostics, and support.
- [ ] Make privacy notices and consent controls concise, accessible, and
      understandable; avoid bundled or pre-checked consent.

## United Kingdom gates

- [ ] Determine whether a UK representative is required and, if so, appoint one
      in writing and publish its non-residential contact details.
- [ ] Determine whether an ICO data-protection fee/registration obligation applies.
- [ ] Document each restricted transfer using the current UK transfer framework,
      including transfer-risk assessment and IDTA/Addendum or another lawful
      mechanism where required.
- [ ] Implement UK data-subject rights, complaint routing to the ICO, and current
      breach assessment/notification procedure.
- [ ] Review cookies, local storage, authentication, analytics, and electronic
      communications under PECR and current consent guidance.
- [ ] Assess UK consumer, unfair-terms, digital-content/service, subscription,
      cancellation, refund, and pricing obligations.
- [ ] Assess whether the Online Safety Act applies to any user-to-user, search,
      public-content, or other regulated service feature; do not infer exclusion.
- [ ] Review accessibility obligations for the app, website, support, and any
      e-commerce flow.

## Ireland gates

- [ ] Complete every EEA activation gate, including EU representative assessment,
      transfer mechanisms, rights, incident timing, DPIA, and AI Act role analysis.
- [ ] Identify the competent supervisory authority and whether the Irish Data
      Protection Commission would be the relevant authority for the actual
      establishment/targeting structure.
- [ ] Review Irish ePrivacy/electronic-marketing, consumer, subscription, tax,
      trader, and accessibility requirements.
- [ ] Verify complaint, rights, and breach intake can be handled for Irish users.

## Publication and testing

- [ ] Publish distinct UK and Ireland/EEA disclosures where their mechanisms,
      authorities, representatives, or rights differ.
- [ ] Ensure Michigan forum/law language preserves mandatory local rights.
- [ ] Provide representative, controller, DPO, and authority details only after
      they are verified; never publish a residential address.
- [ ] Reconcile App Store availability, App Privacy, privacy manifests, provider
      consent, deletion, and retention.
- [ ] Test accessibility, locale, links, dates, time zones, currency, tax, consent,
      rights, and deletion from each country.
- [ ] Record separate activation approvals for the UK and Ireland.

## Current blockers

1. Representatives, territorial scope, controller roles, and authority routing
   are unresolved.
2. International-transfer mechanisms and provider evidence are incomplete.
3. Rights, deletion, retention, diagnostics, and incident operations are not ready.
4. Consumer, PECR/ePrivacy, Online Safety, accessibility, and AI assessments are
   incomplete.
5. No approved non-residential public address/representative solution exists.
