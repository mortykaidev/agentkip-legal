# European Economic Area Activation Checklist

Status: **INACTIVE / BLOCKED**

Activation owner: Brandon

Last source check: 2026-07-15

This checklist covers the European Union plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway.
Member-state consumer, age, communications, employment, and other local rules may
add obligations beyond the shared EU/EEA framework.

## GDPR scope and governance

- [ ] Confirm territorial scope for each offered service and monitoring activity.
- [ ] Identify the controller, joint-controller, processor, and independent-
      controller roles for AgentKip, Noggin, providers, integrations, and users.
- [ ] Determine whether an Article 27 EEA representative is required; if so,
      appoint one in writing and publish verified non-residential contact details.
- [ ] Determine whether a data protection officer is required and record the
      rationale/contact where applicable.
- [ ] Complete records of processing with purpose, categories, recipients,
      transfers, retention, and safeguards.
- [ ] Select and document a lawful basis for every purpose. Keep contract,
      legitimate interests, legal obligation, and freely given consent distinct.
- [ ] Identify Article 9 special-category or Article 10 data paths and block them
      unless a valid condition and necessary safeguards are established.

## Individual rights and transparency

- [ ] Implement access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability,
      consent withdrawal, and complaint workflows as applicable.
- [ ] Explain automated/AI processing, recipients, retention, transfers, and
      material consequences in layered, accessible notices.
- [ ] Assess whether any solely automated decision produces legal or similarly
      significant effects; if it does, add the required basis, safeguards, human
      intervention, and contest mechanism before activation.
- [ ] Verify identity without collecting disproportionate new information.
- [ ] Track statutory response timing and extensions using current rules.

## Risk, security, and incidents

- [ ] Complete a data protection impact assessment for provider transfer,
      profiling, monitoring, sensitive content, and autonomous action risks when
      the Article 35 threshold is met; otherwise record the screened rationale.
- [ ] Implement privacy by design/default: diagnostics and device-context
      transmission off by default, least-privilege integrations, data
      minimization, short retention, and fail-closed approvals.
- [ ] Establish processor instructions/contracts, access controls, auditability,
      deletion, backup treatment, and breach escalation.
- [ ] Add EEA supervisory-authority and affected-person breach analysis to the
      incident plan, including the current 72-hour authority-notification rule
      when its threshold applies.

## International transfers

- [ ] Map each transfer outside the EEA, including support/admin access and
      onward transfers by subprocessors.
- [ ] Confirm adequacy, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where valid and applicable,
      Standard Contractual Clauses, Binding Corporate Rules, or another current
      mechanism for each transfer.
- [ ] Complete transfer impact/risk assessments and supplementary safeguards where
      required.
- [ ] Explain transfers and how to obtain relevant safeguards in the privacy notice.
- [ ] Do not use consent as a routine substitute for missing transfer safeguards.

## AI, platform, consumer, and accessibility gates

- [ ] Classify AgentKip/Noggin roles and use cases under the EU AI Act and document
      provider/deployer, transparency, literacy, prohibited-practice, and risk
      obligations with their applicable dates.
- [ ] Ensure users know when they interact with AI and can review or stop
      consequential actions.
- [ ] Assess the Digital Services Act only against actual intermediary/platform
      features; document applicability or exclusion.
- [ ] Review consumer, unfair-terms, digital-content/service, withdrawal,
      subscription, pricing, tax/VAT, trader, and dispute-resolution requirements.
- [ ] Assess the European Accessibility Act and member-state implementation for
      the app, site, support, and e-commerce flow.
- [ ] Review ePrivacy/cookie/local-storage/electronic-marketing rules per launch
      member state.

## Activation evidence

- [ ] Country list and member-state variance review.
- [ ] Representative/DPO/controller contacts with no residential address.
- [ ] Approved EEA notice, Terms adaptations, cookie controls, and rights portal.
- [ ] Provider register, SCC/transfer evidence, DPIA/ROPA, and incident contacts.
- [ ] App Store regional availability, privacy answers, manifests, age rating, and
      reviewer notes reconciled.
- [ ] Locale, accessibility, consent, rights, deletion, approval, and failure tests.
- [ ] Brandon's dated activation approval and evidence commit.

## Current blockers

All governance, rights, transfer, AI Act, consumer, accessibility, and operational
items above remain blocked. EEA distribution must remain disabled.
